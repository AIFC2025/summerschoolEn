COURSE Introduction

Mathematics for Machine Learning

This course covers probabilistic theories for analyzing machine learning models. Topics include fundamentals of probability metric spaces, mean-field models for supervised learning neural networks, particle system models for attention mechanisms, and stochastic analysis of diffusion generative models.
Instructors: Zhenjie Ren (Université Paris-Saclay); Lei Li, Jingwei Liang (Shanghai Jiao Tong University)

Frontier Applications of Machine Learning

Explore cutting-edge applications of machine learning in diverse scenarios, including deep learning, large-scale models (e.g., Transformers), and embodied intelligence.
Instructors: Yaoyu Zhang, Zheng Ma (Shanghai Jiao Tong University)

AI for Science

Discusses the interdisciplinary use of AI in natural and social sciences, including computational cosmology, computational biology, and social network analysis—driving cross-disciplinary innovation.
Instructors: Hao Wu, Yuguang Wang, Qiaoqiao Ding (Shanghai Jiao Tong University)

AI Governance and Ethics (Courses delivered in English)

Analyzes the impact of AI on global governance, privacy, and algorithmic fairness. Explores frameworks for building sustainable and responsible AI ecosystems.
Instructors: Éric Brousseau (Université Paris Sciences et Lettres); Emmanuel Roberto Goffi (Global AI Ethics Institute, EPITA School of Engineering and Computer Science)

AI Innovation and Entrepreneurship

Combines hands-on entrepreneurial experience with theoretical insights to help students understand the full lifecycle of AI products—from R&D to market launch. Topics include business models, venture capital, and competitive strategies.
Instructors: Yapeng Yu (Co-founder, Tushen Zhihe); Shuai Zhang (CEO, Upsail AI); Jiachen He (Founder, Affine.pro)

Group Project Practice

Participants will collaborate in teams to tackle real-world AI challenges across five thematic areas. Each team will be mentored by a dedicated instructor and present their outcomes at the Demo Day. Topics include but are not limited to:
1. AI + Mathematical Models: Probabilistic metric spaces, mean-field neural networks, particle systems in attention models
2. AI + Scientific Research: Computational cosmology, bioinformatics, social network analysis
3. AI + Frontier Applications: Foundation models, intelligent manufacturing, fintech, medical AI
4. AI + Governance & Society: AI ethics, social sciences, privacy protection
5. AI + Entrepreneurship & Innovation: AI-powered business intelligence, product development, go-to-market strategies
